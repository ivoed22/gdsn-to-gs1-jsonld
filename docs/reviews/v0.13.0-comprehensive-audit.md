## Executive summary

v0.13.0 adds the Product Passport Builder in **minimal-schema prototype mode**.
It wraps GS1 Web Vocabulary JSON-LD into a prototype Product Passport JSON-LD
envelope and validates it against the committed built-in minimal schema, reusing
the v0.12.x validator (no duplicated logic). The feature is correctly scoped:
deterministic, offline, clearly prototype/reference, with no compliance claims.

The main risk in this release was **information architecture** — the overview
now hosts seven workflows. This audit applied a contained IA improvement
(grouping + labels + a start-here cue) rather than a redesign. No critical or
major findings. Release-ready.

## Minor findings

### MF-1 — Seven-workflow overview density (ADDRESSED)

At seven workflows the previous 4+3 grid read as one dense block with no
narrative. **Applied:** workflows are reordered into three intent groups, each
with a heading, and rendered at most three cards per row:

1. **Start here — convert product data:** Convert GDSN XML (with a "recommended
   starting point" cue).
2. **Explore & review mappings:** Explore GS1 Web Vocabulary, Generate Mapping
   Candidates, Standards Review.
3. **Prototype linked data & Product Passports:** Create JSON-LD Prototype,
   Validate Product Passport Sources, Build Product Passport Prototype.

### MF-2 — "Prototype" naming overlap (ADDRESSED)

"Create JSON-LD Prototype" and "Build Product Passport Prototype" both contain
"Prototype." **Applied:** the Create card copy now says "manually author GS1 Web
Vocabulary JSON-LD"; the Build card copy now says "wrap GS1 Web Vocabulary
JSON-LD (converter or manual-builder output) into a prototype Product Passport
envelope." The two are now clearly a *produce-vocabulary* vs *wrap-into-passport*
pair, and they sit in the same group so the relationship is legible.

## UI/UX findings

- Workflow cards remain calm and evidence-oriented; markers (XML/VOC/MAP/SDR/
  LD/PP/PB) are distinct with their own accent colors (added `mode-pb`).
- The Build workflow's four tabs (Input / Settings / Output / Validation Report)
  map cleanly to the task; empty states and parse-error states are present.
- Governance warnings are preserved everywhere; the grouping did **not** remove
  or hide any warning.

## Information architecture findings

- Grouping into three intents is the smallest change that makes seven workflows
  legible; it caps each row at three cards, reducing per-row density.
- **Responsive note:** Streamlit `st.columns` manage their own stacking on
  narrow viewports; forcing an exact 2-column layout at ~803 px via CSS is not
  reliably achievable without JavaScript. Capping rows at three cards is the
  practical mitigation and is now in place. The 900 px / 640 px CSS breakpoints
  for hero, traceability rail, and summary grids remain.
- The traceability rail still represents the converter evidence spine
  (XML → BMS/XPath → JSON-LD → SDR); the entry narrative now covers the full
  grouped set including the Product Passport Builder, so the rail need not grow.

## Accessibility findings

- Group labels are real text headings; markers have text equivalents via card
  titles; warnings are text + color (not color-only).
- No regressions introduced. Custom warning blocks could still gain
  `role="note"` (carried forward from the v0.12.1 audit as a non-blocker).

## Backend findings

`product_passport_builder.py`:
- Deterministic by default; `generatedAt` omitted unless explicitly supplied
  (test-covered).
- `prototypeNotice` always present; extraction tolerant of direct-`gtin`,
  language-value `productName`, brand string/object, and Digital Link `@id`.
- Invalid/non-object JSON rejected with readable `ValueError`.
- `include_source_gs1_jsonld` toggle works; validation reuses
  `product_passport_sources.validate_product_passport_json` (no duplication).
- Output bytes stable; writer uses `mkdir(parents=True)` + fixed filenames under
  the caller-provided directory; no network; no source-manifest mutation; no
  mapping writes.
